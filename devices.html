Custom vs. Off-the-Shelf

When to Choose Custom vs. Adapt Existing Standards

A practical guide to help you decide when a custom connector is the right investment for your medical device program.

Consideration Custom Solution (Gii Recommends) Off-the-Shelf Adaptation
Connector Fit Your device geometry requires a unique form factor Standard form factors work with your design
Contact Count You need 10+ contacts in a tight space Fewer than 10 contacts or standard configurations
Performance Specs High-voltage, high-frequency, or EMI/RFI immunity is critical Standard electrical performance is sufficient
Volume & Cost Volume > 10K units/year; custom tooling justified Low-to-medium volume; cost-sensitive
Biocompatibility Specialized materials or coatings required (e.g., PTFE, PFA) Standard commercial polymers acceptable
Single-Use Requirement Single-use breakaway or sterilization-integrated design Connector reused across multiple procedures
Regulatory Strategy Predicate path requires custom solution; 510(k) advantage Leveraging established predicate devices
Time-to-Market Can absorb 6–9 month engineering & tooling phase Need to launch in < 6 months
Supply Chain Control Want sole-source, dedicated manufacturing partnership Prefer multi-source, vendor-agnostic components

Gii's Recommendation: When in doubt, start with an engineering conversation. We'll help you evaluate cost, timeline, and regulatory implications — then recommend the best path forward, whether that's custom engineering or smart adaptation of existing standards.
