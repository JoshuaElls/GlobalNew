Global Interconnect manufactures precision assemblies that connect medical devices or industrial consoles to surgical handpieces, measurement devices, and diagnostic imaging systems. Every product is built to zero-failure standards — rigorously tested and validated to perform in the most demanding clinical environments.

Category 01

Custom Medical Connectors

From push-pull to screw-locking, Gii manufactures connector housings overmolded with precision contacts — compatible with LEMO/Fischer standards or fully proprietary architectures.

  • Non-locking push-pull designs
  • Bayonet & screw-locking styles
  • 1–30+ contacts per design
  • LEMO/Fischer compatible
  • Biocompatible materials
  • Single-use & reusable options
  • Custom overmolded housings
  • Fully proprietary architectures
Category 02

RF Ablation Assemblies

Complete radiofrequency ablation assemblies for pain management, cardiac ablation, and surgical cutting — all IEC 60601 compliant and 100% electrically tested before shipment.

  • Overmolded banana plug configs
  • Push-pull RF connectors (1–30+ contacts)
  • Breakaway connectors for pain probes
  • High-voltage rated per IEC 60601
  • Low-leakage RF insulation
  • 100% electrical test before ship

  • Ultrasound & imaging cables
  • Multi-lumen catheter assemblies
  • RF & microwave delivery
  • Sterilization-rated flexible tubing
  • Shielded EMI/RFI designs

Pain Management Devices

Decision Guide

Custom Solution vs. Off-the-Shelf

A practical side-by-side to help you evaluate the right path for your program.

✦ Choose Custom (Gii Recommends)

When standard solutions fall short of your requirements

FitUnique form factor required
Contacts10+ contacts in tight space
PerformanceHigh-voltage, RF, or EMI-critical
Volume>10K units/year; tooling justified
MaterialPTFE, PFA, or biocomp. coatings needed
Use TypeSingle-use breakaway or sterilized
Regulatory510(k) predicate advantage
TimelineCan absorb 6–9 mo. eng. phase
SupplySole-source partnership preferred

Off-the-Shelf Adaptation

When standard components can meet your needs

FitStandard form factors work fine
ContactsFewer than 10, standard configs
PerformanceStandard electrical specs sufficient
VolumeLow-to-medium; cost-sensitive
MaterialCommercial polymers acceptable
Use TypeConnector reused across procedures
RegulatoryLeveraging established predicates
TimelineNeed to launch in <6 months
SupplyMulti-source, vendor-agnostic OK

Gii's Recommendation: When in doubt, start with an engineering conversation. We'll help you evaluate cost, timeline, and regulatory implications — then recommend the best path forward, whether that's custom engineering or smart adaptation of existing standards.
